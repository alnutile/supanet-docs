---
title: Card boards
description: Free-form boards for brainstorming and prioritizing ideas with movable cards.
---

A **card board** is a canvas where you dump ideas as cards and drag them to rank by priority. Unlike Kanban boards with fixed columns, cards go anywhere on the board — position is the priority. It's deliberately low-friction for quick brainstorming and planning.

## Why card boards are useful

Card boards give you a space to capture and organize thoughts fast. You can:

- **Dump ideas quickly** — double-click the canvas to add a card and type an idea.
- **Rank by priority** — drag cards around; higher and more left means higher priority.
- **Collaborate in real time** — multiple people can move cards at once and see each other's changes instantly.
- **Have the assistant help** — ask Claude to brainstorm ideas as cards, or check what's on the board and suggest changes.
- **Keep ideas organized** — save card boards to collections alongside artifacts and to-dos so the team can find them.
- **Persist your thinking** — changes save automatically as you work.

## Where to find card boards

On the sidebar, find **Cards** under **Assets** (next to Whiteboards). You'll see a list of all your boards.

## Creating a board

Click **New board** to create one. You can name it and start adding ideas right away. The canvas is blank at first — you decide what goes on it.

## Adding cards

On a board, you have two ways to add cards:

- **Double-click empty space** — the canvas opens a new card at that spot, ready for you to type.
- **Click the "+ Card" button** — adds a card near the center of the visible area.

Each card has:

- A **title bar** — drag it to move the card anywhere on the canvas.
- **Text area** — type or edit your idea.
- **Color swatch** — click it to pick a color (yellow, pink, green, blue, purple, gray) so ideas at a glance are recognizable.
- **Delete button (×)** — removes the card (no undo — be careful).

## Prioritizing cards

Cards have no fixed columns or lanes. Instead, **position is priority**:

- Cards higher on the board rank higher.
- Cards to the left rank higher than those to the right (reading order).
- Drag the title bar to move a card anywhere.

When you chat with a board or the assistant reads it, cards are listed top-to-bottom so the ranking is clear.

## Real-time collaboration

When a board is open:

- You see other team members' **movements** as they drag cards in real time.
- Changes are **synced instantly** — when someone adds, moves, or deletes a card, you see it right away.
- Your work is **saved automatically** as you move cards, so there's no "save" button to click.
- If someone updates the board from another device or via the assistant, the changes appear on your canvas.

## Sharing and visibility

Each board has a visibility setting:

- **Private** — only you (and admins) can see and edit it.
- **Workspace** — everyone on your team can see it and move cards together.

Toggle visibility in the board header. Team members can only edit a board if you've shared it as workspace-visible.

## Collections and context

You can file card boards into **collections** — the same named groups you use for artifacts, files, and to-dos. From the Card boards list page, select boards and click **Add to collection** to file them.

When you use a collection in Chat (via the 📚 picker), the assistant can see the boards in that collection and help you edit them. The assistant can also create a board and file it into a collection as part of a larger task.

## Chat with a board

When you have a board open, you can:

- Click **Chat** (in the sidebar) to open a conversation about the board.
- Tell the assistant to add ideas — "brainstorm 5 marketing angles" or "what's the next step?".
- The assistant reads the board's current state and adds cards in place instead of creating a new board.
- Ask "what's the top priority?" and the assistant reads the board's ranking.

## Automation and the API

The assistant and MCP can also manage boards: they can create them, list them, read the current state, and add cards — all without you touching the UI. This means an agent can run a weekly brainstorm and dump results as cards, and your team always has the latest ideas ranked.
