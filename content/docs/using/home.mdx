The **Home** page is your workspace at a glance. When you log in to SupaNet, you land here and see what's been going on, who is doing what, and what needs your attention — all without having to jump between different pages.

## Why the Home dashboard is useful

Home gives you a fast, personalized view of your workspace. You can:

- **See stats at a glance** — how many to-dos are open, how many artifacts and files you have, and how much activity happened this week.
- **Track completion** — a progress bar shows what percent of your to-dos are done.
- **Watch the trend** — a 14-day activity chart shows when the team is busiest.
- **Scan recent activity** — a live feed of the latest events (chats, files, to-dos, etc.) so you know what just happened.
- **See your open to-dos** — the most urgent tasks, right there, and you can check them off without leaving the page.
- **Build your own widgets** — compose custom tiles (counts, lists, or charts) from any of your data so the dashboard shows exactly what matters to you.

## The two views: Overview and Explore

Home has two tabs:

### Overview

The default view when you log in. It's a "what's going on" dashboard with:

- **Quick actions** — buttons to start a chat, upload a file, or create an agent.
- **Pinned artifacts** — your most important artifacts in a grid (if you have any pinned). Shows up to 12 artifacts sorted by most recently updated, with a preview of each.
- **Stat tiles** — counts of open to-dos (with a completion bar), artifacts, files, and events this week.
- **Activity trend chart** — the last 14 days of activity as a bar chart, so you can spot when the team is most active.
- **Recent activity feed** — the 7 most recent events, with a colored badge showing the event type (e.g., `artifact.created`, `message.received`) and a summary of what happened.
- **Your to-dos** — your top open tasks, with due dates if set. Hover over a to-do and click the checkbox to mark it done right there.
- **Your custom widgets** — tiles you (or the AI) have composed to show exactly what you want to see.

### Explore

The original feature index. Click the **Explore** tab to see all the things you can do in SupaNet — collections, artifacts, tables, whiteboards, and more — with quick-jump buttons and a search bar.

## Creating custom widgets

Widgets are the most powerful part of Home. They let you build a dashboard without any code.

Click the **Add widget** button in the "Your widgets" section. A panel opens where you describe what you want to see, and the assistant builds it for you.

Examples:

- "My open to-dos as a count"
- "Artifacts I added today as a list"
- "A chart of my activity this month"
- "My latest 5 files"

The assistant picks the right kind (a count, a list, or a bar chart), the right data source (to-dos, artifacts, files, links, collections, or activity), and any filters you want. When you confirm, the widget appears on your dashboard and syncs live across your devices.

### What widgets can show

A widget is made up of three things:

- **Kind** — how to render it:
  - `stat`: a single big number (count of items)
  - `list`: recent rows from a table, shown as a list with titles and timestamps
  - `chart`: a bar chart of activity per day (last 14 days)
- **Source** — the data table:
  - `todos`: your to-do list
  - `artifacts`: your saved documents and code
  - `files`: files you uploaded
  - `links`: bookmarked links
  - `collections`: named groups of items
  - `activity`: the activity log of what happened in your workspace
- **Spec** — optional filters:
  - `window`: time filter (`today`, `7d`, `30d`, or `all`)
  - `mine`: show only rows you own (default is off, so workspace items show)
  - `status`: for to-dos only, filter by `open` or `done`
  - `limit`: for list widgets, how many rows to show (default 5, max 20)

### Managing widgets

Once a widget is on your dashboard:

- **Remove it** — hover over the widget and click the × button in the top right.
- **Edit it** — delete it and create a new one with different settings. (Direct editing is coming soon.)

Widgets are personal — they only show on your dashboard. A widget you create doesn't appear on anyone else's Home page.

## Real-time updates

Everything on Home updates live:

- When a new to-do is created, the count goes up and it appears in your list.
- When activity happens, new events appear at the top of the feed.
- When a team member creates a to-do or artifact, you see the counts update.
- When you (or the AI) add a widget, it appears on your dashboard right away, even if you are on another device.

## Tips for using Home

- **Use Home as your entry point** — check it when you log in to get your bearings.
- **Pin what matters** — create widgets for things you care about. A product team might pin "my open to-dos" and "artifacts I shared this week". A manager might pin "team activity chart" and "open to-dos in the Goals collection".
- **Check the trend chart** — glance at the activity chart to see when the team is busy and when things are quiet.
- **Mark to-dos as done from Home** — you don't have to go to the To-dos page. Click the checkbox on a task and it is marked done right there.
- **Switch to Explore** — when you want to browse all your stuff or search globally, click the **Explore** tab.
